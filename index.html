Stanford University - From Languages to Information Coding NLP

Python Regexes

The first assignment started off by making us enforce spam rules in Python with regular expressions. It was a small building block that I was not sure would be very helpful outside of niche situations, but as the class progressed I realized how important being able to use regexes were in natural langauge processing!

Classification

The next couple of assignments looked a lot more like what I expected. It started with us implementing a Naive-Bayes classifier and then Logistic Regression. For the first time, I was able to use my prior coding knowledge to create something that has actual value outside of the classroom.

Information Retreival

I also implemented some basic information retreival systems such as indexing and boolean search. It was rewarding both in understanding about classification and information retreival and in cementing my coding fundamentals.

Prediction Models

By the midpoint of the course, the application of the concepts we were learning was obvious. The last few assignments had us create prediction models using word counts and cosine similarity scores.

Neural Networks

While rather basic, these concepts helped lead us into neural networks. The neural networks we created vastly outperformed any other models we had created and excited me to learn more about neural networks and deep learning!

Chatbot

The final assignment combined a lot of the skills we had learned up to this point. I worked to create a chatbot that was both dynamic and functional. We learned about a lot of the common frameworks and methods programmers employ when creating chat bot routines. Perhaps more importantly, however, the course introduced a wide swath of ethical questions to consider as well. Here I learned that it was not just important to make something that works, but also to make something that will improve the lives of users.

Unknown Input

The best part of the course was the project at the end. It again combined a lot of the concepts we had learned about, but also introduced uncontrolled variables such as bad input and required that we create a bot that could be packaged and distributed to the real world (i.e. we had to plan for a lot of different situations).

Coding Together

I keep saying we because the project was to be completed with a team, so it was another opportunity to demonstrate my teamwork skills and help lead when others were falling behind. It was a great way to bring together everything we had learned to create a robust and realistic deliverable that we were all proud of by the end.

Stanford University - Writing & Rhetoric 2: Ethics and AI Research Environmentalism

Proposal

My proposal idea came from my interest in sustainable energy. I wanted to take this opportunity to pursue my own interests and present them in an academic and professional format that I knew could properly convey my sentiments. This process began by writing a proposal which you can view here.

Finding My Sources

After my proposal, the next part of my process was conducting my research on smart grids. I knew very little about smart grids prior to this project, but by the end I had learned many of the intricacies and difficulties faced by proponents of smart grids. I was able to comb through a relatively dense number of sources by using key words when searching and skimming through the abstracts and sections of ones I opened. The ability to process a lot of this material and data quickly aided me greatly in selecting great sources.

Spotting Gold

Once I had selected my sources, the next challenge came in choosing the information that I wanted to use. I wanted to convey the main ideas and ways to go about smart grids but also infuse some of the finer details to create a convincing argument. This proved to be a longer process than I imagined, but I thoroughly enjoyed piecing together relevant information from different sources to build my argument and position.

Crafting a Concise Message

Figuring which parts of my research to include in my presentation was a rewarding task. I enjoyed using the work I had already compiled to make a presentation that surpassed the standards I had set for myself. While the slides looked clean and slick, there were a few challenges - both technical and personal - that I had to face.

Employing Multimedia

A good presentation makes use of more than just walls of text. I was able to include photos and infographics that helped strengthen my slides as well as let my audience know what my main points were. Picking good graphics involved looking through a myriad of search results that often didn't suit my presentation, but I enjoyed finding different ways to share my point of view and use different technologies than one would normally expect.

Overcoming Self-Doubt

The biggest challenge I faced, however, was my life-long fear of giving presentations. I had never been the best public speaker growing up, so having to give a presentation that I had spent ten weeks preparing for was a daunting task. Thanks to the lessons I learned and the critical critiques of my instructor, I delivered my presentation with confidence and clarity. I know that this experience has helped me become a better public speaker in all situations today.

Formatting an Oral Argument

The white paper is a document that essentially follows the same format and structure of a verbal presentation, albeit on paper. I was able to use my presentation that had been recorded and transcribed to form the majority of my paper, but the key in making an excellent white paper was having the ability to make the edits and transitions necessary. A lot of the phrases I used in my presentation didn't make much sense as words on paper, so making adjustments was pivotal.

Improvement

This entire research process let me know what is important and not important when people's time is limited. I had to be clear with my messages and still formulate a strong argument. It is clear to me that being thorough in my work and being receptive to feedback is key to having a learning mindset. My ability to speak with confidence and create both strong oral and written arguments has greatly improved since taking this course.
