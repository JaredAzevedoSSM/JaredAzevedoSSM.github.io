Leadership. Passion. Curiosity.

Hi there! I'm Jared Azevedo - an aspiring software engineer with a multicultural and multidiscplinary background. Throughout my life, I have focused and developed around three key values: leadership, passion, and curiosity.

About Me

Family First

The biggest factor in a lot of the life decisions I have made is my family. Being able to provide and take care of them later in life is something I want to have the flexibility to do. It is also important to me that I am able to live up to the expectations they had for themselves (i.e. they have been excellent role models).

The Island I Call Home

When people ask where I come from, I usually reply Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan. However, the truth is I grew up on a small island right across the St. Mary's river from Sault Ste. Marie called Sugar Island. Growing up in a close-knit community was incredibly nice and I consider myself fortunate for that, but it did little to prepare me for the next chapters of my life.

Heading West

One of the defining characteristics of Stanford isn't the history or location, but rather the diverse range of courses they require undergraduates to take. Instead of spending all my time in exactly one field, I have been granted the chance to persue many different interests to create a unique background that truly speaks to who I am and what I want to do in life. It has forced me to have a multidiscplinary background that has helped me view problems from different angles and come up with new solutions some might not see at first.

The Earth Comes First

The major thing I hope to accomplish through my body of work is create sustainable tech. This might take a variety of forms, such as helping with smart grids or creating an entirely new technology. It might also include creating software and hardware that we use in computers that do not consume as much energy or are made of materials that are more environmentally friendly. Either way, my main goal for work is to make technology not just better our lives, but the lives of the generations ahead and the planet we will leave for them to inherit.

After Me

I think it would be hard to achieve the above ambitions without leaving some sort of impact on society. Even with other work that I will enjoy along my journey, I want to something that improves the lives of my peers and everyone around me. Stanford University - PWR 2: Ethics and AI

Placeholder Header

After my proposal, the next part of my process was conducting my research on smart grids. I knew very little about smart grids prior to this project, but by the end I had learned many of the intricacies and difficulties faced by proponents of smart grids. I was able to comb through a relatively dense number of sources by using key words when searching and skimming through the abstracts and sections of ones I opened. The ability to process a lot of this material and data quickly aided me greatly in selecting great sources.

Placeholder Header

Once I had selected my sources, the next challenge came in choosing the information that I wanted to use. I wanted to convey the main ideas and ways to go about smart grids but also infuse some of the finer details to create a convincing argument. This proved to be a longer process than I imagined, but I thoroughly enjoyed piecing together relevant information from different sources to build my argument and position.

Placeholder Header

This entire research process let me know what is important and not important when people's time is limited. I had to be clear with my messages and still formulate a strong argument. It is clear to me that being thorough in my work and being receptive to feedback is key to having a learning mindset. My ability to speak with confidence and create both strong oral and written arguments has greatly improved since taking this course.
